# Layer 2: Process Dimension

**Official Operating Procedures for Digital Government Service Delivery**

---

## Overview

The Process Dimension represents **40% of the GaaS investment framework** and provides the proven, standardized processes for designing, building, operating, and improving digital government services.

**Core Principle:** Digitizing broken processes creates faster failure. Fix the process first, then automate.

---

## Process Frameworks

Layer 2 integrates world-class process methodologies adapted for government context:

- **ITIL v4** - 34 service management practices for operational excellence
- **Agile/Scrum** - Iterative, user-centered service delivery
- **Lean Six Sigma** - Process optimization and continuous improvement
- **DevOps** - Automated, reliable technology operations
- **TBM (Technology Business Management)** - Cost transparency and value measurement
- **COBIT** - IT governance and risk management

## Process Maturity Model

### Level 1: Initial (Ad-hoc)

**Characteristics:**
- Processes undocumented or inconsistent
- Success depends on individual heroics
- Reactive problem-solving
- No measurement or metrics

**Typical Organizations:** Early-stage digital government initiatives

### Level 2: Managed (Repeatable)

**Characteristics:**
- Core processes documented
- Project-level management
- Basic metrics tracked
- Some consistency across teams

**Typical Organizations:** 6-12 months into transformation, pilot services live

### Level 3: Defined (Proactive)

**Characteristics:**
- Enterprise-wide process standards
- Integrated processes across organization
- Continuous improvement culture
- Predictable outcomes

**Typical Organizations:** 18-36 months into transformation, 50+ services operational

### Level 4: Measured (Quantitative)

**Characteristics:**
- Data-driven decision making
- Advanced analytics on process performance
- Process optimization based on metrics
- High reliability and quality

**Typical Organizations:** 3-5 years into transformation, 100+ services, mature operations

### Level 5: Optimizing (Continuous Innovation)

**Characteristics:**
- Continual process innovation
- Automation of routine processes
- Proactive problem prevention
- World-class performance

**Typical Organizations:** Estonia, Singapore, UAE - sustained excellence over 5-10+ years

## Integration with People Dimension

Process and People work as one integrated engine:

**PROCESS provides the METHOD:**
- ITIL defines HOW to operate services (incident, change, problem management)
- Agile defines HOW to deliver services (sprints, user stories, retrospectives)
- TBM defines HOW to measure value (cost allocation, service catalog)
- Zero Trust defines HOW to secure systems (verify everything, trust nothing)

**PEOPLE provide the CAPABILITY:**
- Service managers execute ITIL processes
- Scrum masters facilitate agile delivery
- Financial analysts perform TBM cost modeling
- Security engineers implement Zero Trust architecture

**TOGETHER they deliver:**
- Operational excellence (99.5%+ uptime)
- Rapid service delivery (2-week sprints)
- Cost transparency (know cost-per-service)
- Secure services (Zero Trust foundation)

## Common Pitfalls & Solutions

### Pitfall 1: Process Over People

**Symptom:** Rigid adherence to process without empowering people to adapt

**Solution:** Balance process rigor with team autonomy. ITIL and Agile are frameworks, not straitjackets.

### Pitfall 2: Boiling the Ocean

**Symptom:** Trying to implement all 10 playbooks simultaneously

**Solution:** Phased approach. Start with GSM foundation, add processes incrementally.

### Pitfall 3: Process Theater

**Symptom:** Documented processes that no one follows in practice

**Solution:** Make processes visible, measure compliance, embed in tools (not just documents).

### Pitfall 4: Technology Before Process

**Symptom:** Buying tools before defining processes

**Solution:** Define process first, then select tools that support it (not the other way around).

### Pitfall 5: One-Size-Fits-All

**Symptom:** Copy-pasting Estonia's X-Road or Singapore's Singpass process without adaptation

**Solution:** Adapt best practices to local context (culture, capacity, regulations).

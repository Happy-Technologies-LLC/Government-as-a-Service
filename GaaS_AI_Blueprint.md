## 1. The Goal: From Library to Operating System

* **The "As-Is" (Old GaaS):** A-la-carte Tiers 0-5. A "library" of useful, but disconnected, assets.
* **The "To-Be" (New GaaS):** A single, holistic "operating system" for a government.

Your job is to read, understand, synthesize, and rewrite the old content to fit this new, superior model.

This is the model you must build, as defined in `Problem_Solution.md`. It has two integrated views:

**View 1: The GaaS Architectural Framework (The Static Structure)**
* **Layer 0:** The Legal & Policy Foundation

* **Layer 3:** The Transparent Service Portfolio

## 3. Source Material Audit (The "As-Is" Content)

This is the old GaaS framework. Before migrating, you must understand what "knowledge" is in each Tier:

* **`Old Tier 0 (Manifesto)`:** Contains the *vision, philosophy, and guiding principles*. The "why" of the entire framework.
* **`Old Tier 1 (Executive Playbook)`:** Contains *strategic guidance for leaders*, focusing on portfolio management, business cases, and TBM.
* **`Old Tier 2 (Technical Blueprints)`:** Contains *hard technical architectures* (e.g., Digital ID, Payments) AND *organizational design* (e.g., People & Org Architecture). **This Tier must be split up.**
* **`Old Tier 3 (Implementation Playbooks)`:** Contains *process-centric "how-to" guides* (e.g., Agile, ITIL, Service Desk). This is the "how-to" for the *workforce*.
* **`Old Tier 4 (Policy & Legal Templates)`:** Contains *draft legislation and policy documents*. These are the "rules" and "authority."
* **`Old Tier 5 (Community Hub)`:** Contains strategies for *public engagement, feedback, and co-design*.
